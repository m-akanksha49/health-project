Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs)

Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) are medical conditions that are not infectious and typically have a prolonged course. They account for the majority of deaths worldwide and include cardiovascular diseases, cancers, chronic respiratory diseases, and diabetes.

Cardiovascular Diseases

Disorders of the heart and blood vessels including coronary heart disease, cerebrovascular disease, and hypertension.

  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol
  • Diabetes
  • Smoking
  • Obesity

Cancers

Abnormal cell growth with potential to invade or spread to other parts of the body.

  • Tobacco use
  • Unhealthy diet
  • Physical inactivity
  • Alcohol consumption
  • Environmental factors

Diabetes

Chronic disease that occurs when the pancreas does not produce enough insulin or when the body cannot effectively use the insulin it produces.

  • Overweight/obesity
  • Unhealthy diet
  • Physical inactivity
  • Family history
  • Age

Chronic Respiratory Diseases

Diseases of the airways and other structures of the lung, including asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

  • Tobacco smoking
  • Air pollution
  • Occupational chemicals and dusts
  • Frequent lower respiratory infections
